Alan Turing
Alan Turing & Cryptography
Alan Turing was a British mathematician and computer scientist who led a team of cryptographers at Bletchley Park during World War II. They decrypted the code of the Enigma cipher machine and designed a machine called the "Bombe". Turing proposed the idea of a "universal machine" and laid the foundations for modern computing. He was persecuted and convicted for being homosexual and committed suicide in 1954. His contributions to cryptography and computing are hugely influential.
